Tandoori Chicken

Description

Tandoori Chicken, a beloved classic in Indian cuisine, captivates with its bold flavors and succulent texture. This iconic dish begins with tender chicken pieces, traditionally marinated in a vibrant blend of yogurt, lemon juice, and an array of aromatic spices. The marinade, infused with ingredients like cumin, coriander, paprika, and garam masala, imparts layers of flavor while tenderizing the meat, ensuring each bite is an explosion of taste.

To achieve its signature smoky flavor and charred exterior, the marinated chicken is traditionally cooked in a tandoor, a clay oven that reaches scorching temperatures. As the chicken roasts in the intense heat, the marinade caramelizes, forming a tantalizing crust while sealing in the chicken's natural juices. The result is a masterpiece of culinary craftsmanship – juicy, tender chicken with a slightly crispy exterior, imbued with the smoky aroma of the tandoor and the nuanced spice of the marinade. Tandoori Chicken isn't just a dish; it's a sensory experience that transports diners to the bustling streets of India, where the tantalizing aroma of spices and sizzling meats fills the air, inviting them to indulge in the rich tapestry of flavors that define Indian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 medium lemon, juiced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 ¼ cups plain yogurt
  • ½ medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger root
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon red food coloring (Optional)
  • 1 teaspoon yellow food coloring (Optional)
  • 2 teaspoons finely chopped cilantro
  • 1 medium lemon, cut into wedges

Steps

  1. Remove and discard skin from chicken pieces. Cut slits into meat and place into a shallow dish. Season chicken on both sides with lemon juice and salt. Let sit for 20 minutes.
  2. Mix yogurt, onion, garlic, garam masala, ginger, and cayenne pepper together in a medium bowl until smooth, then stir in food coloring.
  3. Spread yogurt mixture over chicken, cover, and refrigerate for 6 to 24 hours (the longer the better).
  4. When ready to cook, preheat an outdoor grill for medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate. Remove chicken from marinade. Discard remaining marinade.
  5. Cook chicken on the preheated grill until no longer pink and the juices run clear. An instant-read thermometer inserted near the bone should read 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
  6. Garnish with cilantro and lemon wedges.
